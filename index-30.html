Strategic Case Studies

Barefoot Wines
01 Brand Strategy

How can a wine brand with a reputation problem win in a declining category?

Stop apologizing for being cheap. Start championing every occasion that doesn't need a fancy bottle.

Built monthly cultural intelligence decks — drawing from social, Mintel, and cultural observation — presented directly to the Barefoot client and fed back into creative briefs.

The Problem

Gen Z is drinking less because they feel like they have fewer reasons to drink.

Barefoot sits in a crowded, shrinking category. Gen Z drinks less than any generation before them — driven by the sober-curious movement, cannabis, and fewer reasons to gather. And within that declining market, Barefoot carries an extra burden: the reputation of cheap wine for people who don't know better.

Cultural Tension

Gen Z is redefining what counts as a special occasion.

Traditional milestones are fading. In their place, Gen Z is inventing their own — Nowhere To Wear It parties, Presentation Nights, couch hangs elevated to events. Economic uncertainty pushed them home, and they turned it into a culture: hyper-specific, deeply personal, and entirely self-made.

Category Tension

The wine category abandoned the everyday, even as everyday moments became the only ones worth celebrating.

As Gen Z redefines celebration, wine brands are doubling down on aspiration — chasing premium occasions like the picnic, the bachelorette, the anniversary dinner — trying to shed the "cheap" label by attaching themselves to moments that feel worthy of a nice bottle. But in doing so, they've abandoned the everyday entirely. No one is owning the unspecial moments that define Gen Z.

The Strategy

Proudly own the Unspecial Occasion.

Emptying the lint tray. Everyone hearted your group text. The friend who showed up with snacks, unprompted. These aren't accidents — they're the moments Gen Z is actually living in. While the category chases occasions worth dressing up for, Barefoot should own every occasion that isn't. Make any moment a Barefoot moment.

Brita
02 Brand Strategy

How does a filtration brand earn cultural relevance with a generation that filters nothing?

Gen Z doesn't filter their feelings. Only their water.

Observation

Gen Z has made oversharing an art form.

Gen Z operates on radical authenticity, seen in everything from therapy speak to public trauma dumping. To them, filtering yourself isn't just uncool, it's fake. And that's something they have zero patience for.

Insight

Gen Z doesn't filter their feelings. Only their water.

In a category obsessed with purity and perfection, Brita found an opening in a culture that rejects filtering altogether, becoming the one filter people would actually claim.

Expression

Dat's A Filter Baby

A song built around the one filter worth keeping — landing the brand inside a cultural truth rather than a product claim.

Cultural Intelligence — Barefoot Wines

As part of a brand intelligence report for Barefoot Wines, I tracked cultural signals across categories to identify emerging opportunities for the brand.

01

Death of the Dinner Table

For Gen Z in 2024, snacking isn't just about eating between meals — it's a lifestyle shift where snacks often replace traditional meals entirely. Valuing "little treats" as everyday indulgences, they want small, satisfying bites that offer moments of joy throughout the day. Sippable treats — smoothies, coffees, and milkshakes — have become snacks in their own right, offering quick, easy enjoyment. This continued shift reflects a broader trend toward flexible, casual eating where convenience and small moments of enjoyment lead the way, allowing activities to dictate meals rather than the reverse.

So What

As meals become simpler and more focused on casual convenience, fit into these snackified moments — offering an approachable, enjoyable pairing for Gen Z's little treats throughout the day.

Cross-category indicators

the rise of tv dinners girl dinner is just snacktime getting yourself a little treat is self care

02

Fun Has A Whole New Meaning For Gen Z

For Gen Z, fun has shifted from packed clubs to more intentional, meaningful gatherings rooted in shared interests and connections. They're gravitating toward spaces that offer depth — like chess clubs, run clubs, and fan communities — where they can bond over common passions. Wine bars are a natural extension of this mindset: Gen Z appreciates the relaxed, social vibe, enjoying the slow pace of savoring wine in a stylish setting. Whether connecting over their favorite artists or hobbies, it's not just about the activity or the wine itself — it's about creating a welcoming, curated atmosphere for shared experiences that feel intimate, inclusive, and authentic.

So What

Bring the wine bar vibe home. Since Gen Z loves the relaxed social atmosphere of wine bars and values the comfort of at-home gatherings, Barefoot can deliver that connected experience right in their living rooms.

Cross-category indicators

a new kind of club growing popularity of wine bars

03

Value Brands Are Having A Major Moment

As Americans remain concerned about inflation, some brands are stepping up as champions of affordability, responding to what people really need and want right now. The appeal of ease and simplicity is making a comeback, with brands tapping into the love for a good deal and laid-back vibe. AriZona Iced Tea, Costco, and Chili's are rising in popularity by delivering on value, consistency, and approachability.

So What

With many Americans viewing rising costs as a top concern, now is the perfect time for Barefoot to spotlight its affordable, accessible appeal.

Cross-category indicators

AriZona is proudly $0.99 @MiddleClassFancy satirizes and celebrates the good ol' American vibe Chili's keeps it self aware and casual
